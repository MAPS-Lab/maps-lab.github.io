Scene Flow Estimation With 4D Automotive Radar

Background: Scene flow allows autonomous vehicles to reason about the arbitrary motion of multiple independent objects which is the key to long-term mobile autonomy. While estimating the scene flow from LiDAR has progressed recently, it remains largely unknown how to estimate the scene flow from a 4D radar - an increasingly popular automotive sensor for its robustness against adverse weather and lighting conditions. Compared with the LiDAR point clouds, radar data are drastically sparser, noisier and in much lower resolution. Annotated datasets for radar scene flow are also in absence and costly to acquire in the real world. Before us, no prior works have been proposed for radar scene flow estimation.
Goal: i) Develop self- and cross-modal supervised learning framework for 4D radar scene flow estimation. ii) Accomplish satisfactory scene flow testing performance without the need of any annotation efforts during training. iii) Support multiple downstream tasks, e.g., motion segmentation, ego-motion estimation, and multi-object tracking, with estimated radar scene flow.
Output: F. Ding, Z. Pan, Y. Deng, J. Deng and C. X. Lu. Self-Supervised Scene Flow Estimation With 4-D Automotive Radar. In IEEE RA-L&IROS, 2022.  PDF  CODE  VIDEO  PAGE
